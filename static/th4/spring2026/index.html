Reading

Romans 6:16-8:17

Chapter 6

P1 NIV 16 Don't you know that when you offer yourselves to someone as obedient slaves, you are slaves of the one you obey—whether you are slaves to sin, which leads to death, or to obedience, which leads to righteousness?

P1 17 But thanks be to God that, though you used to be slaves to sin, you have come to obey from your heart the pattern of teaching that has now claimed your allegiance.

P1 18 You have been set free from sin and have become slaves to righteousness.

P2 NASB 19 I am speaking in human terms because of the weakness of your flesh. For just as you presented the parts of your body as slaves to impurity and to lawlessness, resulting in further lawlessness, so now present your body's parts as slaves to righteousness, resulting in sanctification.

P2 20 When you were slaves to sin, you were free from the control of righteousness.

P2 21 What benefit did you reap at that time from the things you are now ashamed of? Those things result in death!

P2 22 But now that you have been set free from sin and have become slaves of God, the benefit you reap leads to holiness, and the result is eternal life.

P2 23 For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord.

Chapter 7

P3 1 Do you not know, brothers and sisters—for I am speaking to those who know the law—that the law has authority over someone only as long as that person lives?

P3 2 For example, by law a married woman is bound to her husband as long as he is alive, but if her husband dies, she is released from the law that binds her to him.

P3 3 So then, if she has sexual relations with another man while her husband is still alive, she is called an adulteress. But if her husband dies, she is released from that law and is not an adulteress if she marries another man.

P4 4 So, my brothers and sisters, you also died to the law through the body of Christ, that you might belong to another, to him who was raised from the dead, in order that we might bear fruit for God.

P4 HCSB 5 For when we were in the flesh, the sinful passions operated through the law in every part of us and bore fruit for death.

P4 6 But now we have been released from the law, since we have died to what held us, so that we may serve in the new way of the Spirit and not in the old letter of the law.

P5 7 What shall we say then? Is the Law sin? Far from it! On the contrary, I would not have come to know sin except through the Law; for I would not have known about coveting if the Law had not said, "You shall not covet."

P5 8 But sin, taking an opportunity through the commandment, produced in me coveting of every kind; for apart from the Law sin is dead.

P5 9 I was once alive apart from the Law; but when the commandment came, sin came to life, and I died;

P5 10 and this commandment, which was to result in life, proved to result in death for me;

P5 11 for sin, taking an opportunity through the commandment, deceived me, and through it, killed me.

P6 ESV 12 So the law is holy, and the commandment is holy and righteous and good.

P6 13 Therefore did that which is good become a cause of death for me? Far from it! Rather it was sin, in order that it might be shown to be sin by bringing about my death through that which is good, so that through the commandment sin would become utterly sinful.

P7 14 For we know that the Law is spiritual, but I am fleshly, sold into bondage to sin.

P7 15 For I do not understand what I am doing; for I am not practicing what I want to do, but I do the very thing I hate.

P7 16 However, if I do the very thing I do not want to do, I agree with the Law, that the Law is good.

P7 17 But now, no longer am I the one doing it, but sin that dwells in me.

P7 18 For I know that good does not dwell in me, that is, in my flesh; for the willing is present in me, but the doing of the good is not.

P7 19 For the good that I want, I do not do, but I practice the very evil that I do not want.

P7 20 But if I do the very thing I do not want, I am no longer the one doing it, but sin that dwells in me.

P8 21 I find then the principle that evil is present in me, the one who wants to do good.

P8 22 For I joyfully agree with the law of God in the inner person,

P8 23 but I see a different law in the parts of my body waging war against the law of my mind, and making me a prisoner of the law of sin, the law which is in my body's parts.

P8 24 Wretched man that I am! Who will set me free from this body of death?

P8 25 Thanks be to God through Jesus Christ our Lord! So then, on the one hand I myself with my mind am serving the law of God, but on the other, with my flesh the law of sin.

Chapter 8

P9 1 Therefore there is now no condemnation at all for those who are in Christ Jesus.

P9 2 For the law of the Spirit of life in Christ Jesus has set you free from the law of sin and of death.

P9 3 For what the Law could not do, weak as it was through the flesh, God did: sending His own Son in the likeness of sinful flesh and as an offering for sin, He condemned sin in the flesh,

P9 4 so that the requirement of the Law might be fulfilled in us who do not walk according to the flesh but according to the Spirit.

P9 5 For those who are in accord with the flesh set their minds on the things of the flesh, but those who are in accord with the Spirit, the things of the Spirit.

P9 6 For the mind set on the flesh is death, but the mind set on the Spirit is life and peace,

P9 7 because the mind set on the flesh is hostile toward God; for it does not subject itself to the law of God, for it is not even able to do so,

P9 8 and those who are in the flesh cannot please God.

P10 9 However, you are not in the flesh but in the Spirit, if indeed the Spirit of God dwells in you. But if anyone does not have the Spirit of Christ, he does not belong to Him.

P10 10 If Christ is in you, though the body is dead because of sin, yet the spirit is alive because of righteousness.

P10 11 But if the Spirit of Him who raised Jesus from the dead dwells in you, He who raised Christ Jesus from the dead will also give life to your mortal bodies through His Spirit who dwells in you.

P11 12 So then, brothers and sisters, we are under obligation, not to the flesh, to live according to the flesh—

P11 13 for if you are living in accord with the flesh, you are going to die; but if by the Spirit you are putting to death the deeds of the body, you will live.

P11 14 For all who are being led by the Spirit of God, these are sons and daughters of God.

P11 15 For you have not received a spirit of slavery leading to fear again, but you have received a spirit of adoption as sons and daughters by which we cry out, "Abba! Father!"

P11 16 The Spirit Himself testifies with our spirit that we are children of God,

P11 17 and if children, heirs also, heirs of God and fellow heirs with Christ, if indeed we suffer with Him so that we may also be glorified with Him.
