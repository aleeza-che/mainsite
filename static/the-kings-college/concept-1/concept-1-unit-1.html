Clean & Pure: An Introduction to Soul Care - Concept 1 - The Proposal
Genesis 6:3-14, 22 ESV

Backdrop

Leading up to Genesis 6, Yahweh God has not too long ago created the earth in all it's splendor and beauty with perfectly pure human beings Adam and Eve. Just 5 chapters away from that, after the pair committed the first sin, the entire world has gone wrong and corruption is throughout.

While reading this passage, we will underline every reference to a man, his flesh or  heart; a triangle for every reference for God, his heart or his feelings, including pronouns; a circle for every reference for God's Spirit, and a heart for every reference for Noah, including descriptors and pronouns.

Discuss

Genesis 6:3-14,22 ESV
Please do your best to answer the questions as simply as you can based on the text given and not any prior knowledge you may have, even if it's biblical.

Questions with asterisks* may inquire about general definitions and meaning not found in the text, for those you may use your common knowledge.
  • From what we can gather from the text, what was God experiencing on account of what His world had become? (v. 6)
  • What was occurring that made Him feel this way? (v. 5)
  • What is the contrast between Noah and the rest of the world? (v. 9, v. 12)
  • How does God feel about Noah? (v. 8)
  • What does Noah's presence on the earth do for God's perspective of it? (v. 9)
 

Personal Reflection

Genesis 6:3-14,22 ESV
  • What might it have been like for Noah to be so different and to be the only one?
  • In light of God's ability to do anything, i.e., pull Noah into heaven and create a new earth, what do you think God is doing by having Noah be the one to build the ark?
  • Can you think of a time when God required your participation in something He was doing for you?
