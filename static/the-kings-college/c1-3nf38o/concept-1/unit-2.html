1 Peter 3:8-22 Various Translations

Backdrop

In this scripture we find ourselves smack dab in the middle of the 1st century after Christ's death, burial and resurrection. According to church history, in Rome, on July 18 the year 64, a massive fire breaks out, which rages for 9 days and destroys 71% of the city. In order to find someone to blame, Nero, the emperor at this time, pins the cause of the fire on Christians, after which a harsh persecution begins. Peter sends a letter to the various groups of Christians spread about in what is known as present day Turkey. This letter, which we call 1 Peter in our scriptures, warns them of the coming persecution, but encourages them to stay the course. This particular passage prompts them and us to remember the foundation of salvation, especially when times get hard.

While reading this passage, we will underline every reference to a man, his flesh or heart; a triangle for every reference for God, his heart or his feelings, including pronouns; a cross for Christ; a circle for every reference for God's Spirit, and a heart for every reference for Noah or you (what can be considered you), including descriptors and pronouns.
