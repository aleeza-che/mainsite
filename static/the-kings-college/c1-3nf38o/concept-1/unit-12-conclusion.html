Concept 1 - The Proposal

Conclusion

As we conclude concept 1, The Proposal, reflect on the scriptures we have examined. Think about what the bible says the baptism of water is, why it came about and the purpose for it.

Here are some ideas that we’ve encountered:

  • Noah and the flood as the very first baptism. Genesis 6:3-14,22
  • Christ’s submerging and suffering to exchange His life for ours. 1 Peter 3:8-22
  • Our submerging to exchange our dead lives for His life–which may cause us suffering. 1 Peter 3:8-22
  • The second baptism of the Holy Spirit and Fire having two parts. Matthew 3:11–4:1
  • The Fire part of the second baptism referring to Christ's winnowing fork. Matthew 3:11–4:1
  • The participation of Christ in our consequence of grieving God, our participation in the benefit of His sacrifice and of never having grieved God. Romans 6:4-14
  • The necessity of the baptism of the Holy Spirit and Fire. Acts 19:1-12
  • The death represented in our baptism of repentance resulting in our rebirth as something completely new; fused with Christ with a completely new trajectory. 2 Corinthians 5:14-21
  • The proposal of Christ coming after the failure of the flesh proving Christ's devotion as a total act of love. John 21:1-22
  • Resetting the foundation of our faith with God's love and also with Christ's love. Ephesians 3:8-19
  • Viewing Christ's actions not only as duty, but as an expression of his desired relationship with us. John 15:5-23
  • True marriage and God's faithfulness in it. Hosea 2:14-23
  • The commitment to true and full dependency forever. Exodus 19:1-8

I invite you to pray. Ask the Holy Spirit to guide you to your next steps with the Deity.
