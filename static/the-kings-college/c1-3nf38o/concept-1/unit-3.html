Backdrop

This scripture is taken from the account of Jesus' life written by Matthew, one of His close disciples turned apostle. A figure named John the Baptist has devoted his life to living in the desert, eating locusts and wild honey, and immersing sinners into water as a sign of repentance, at the direction of God. He is also spreading the message about the coming of Jesus, which he believes he will see. At this point in the scripture, John has just rebuked the Israeli religious leaders who have come to join in on this sacred display, because of their lack of sincerity.

While reading this passage, we will put a triangle for every reference for God, his heart or his feelings, including pronouns; a cross for Jesus including pronouns; a circle for the Holy Spirit including pronouns; a heart around every reference for you or any followers of Christ, including descriptors and plural pronouns, and underline every reference for fire.
