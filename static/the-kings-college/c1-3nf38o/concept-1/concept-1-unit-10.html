Hosea 2:2-23 Various Translations

Backdrop

In this scripture, the split kingdom Judah and Israel are being warned by yet another prophet of their bad behavior. Judah, representing two tribes—Judah and Benjamin—ruled by kings coming from the line of David; and Israel represented by the other 10 tribes, ruled by various other kings. Around 735 BC, as an illustration to Israel of how they were treating YHWH, this prophet, Hosea, was instructed by YHWH God to marry a prostitute and have children with her. She had 3 children —unknown whether Hosea was the father of all of them or not—named "God Plants" (Jezreel), "Not Loved" or "Not Pitied" and "Not My-People."

While reading this passage, we will put a triangle for every reference for  God, including pronouns; a heart around every reference for Israel, including descriptors and plural pronouns, and an underline for phrases that convey commitment.

The Hebrew translation for the words "My Master" is Ba'al, which is more literally translated as owner. It is used as a play on words that Israel won't even use the term "my master" with God anymore, just so that she will not utter the names of her former gods.
2“Plead with your mother, plead—for she is not my wife,and I am not her husband—that she put away her whoring from her face, and her adultery from between her breasts; 3 lest I strip her naked and make her as in the day she was born,and make her like a wilderness,and make her like a parched land, and kill her with thirst. 4 I will not show my love to her children, because they are the children of adultery. 5 Their mother has been unfaithful and has conceived them in disgrace. She said, ‘I will go after my lovers, who give me my food and my water, my wool and my linen, my olive oil and my drink.’
