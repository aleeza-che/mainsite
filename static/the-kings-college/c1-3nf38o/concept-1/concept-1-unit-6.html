Backdrop

We are referencing another first century letter from Apostle Paul, who, this time is writing his second letter to the the congregation members in the city of Corinth, Ancient Greece. He is defending himself because they don't believe he can be a Spirit-filled apostle because of his many sufferings.

While reading this passage, we will put a triangle for every reference for  God, including pronouns; a cross for  Jesus including pronouns; a circle for every reference of the  Holy Spirit; a line for any reference to the flesh; a heart around every reference for  you or any followers of Christ, including descriptors and plural pronouns, and a star for  unbelievers/potential believers.

Questions with asterisks* may inquire about general definitions and meaning not found in the text, for those you may use your common knowledge.

  • Thinking about the steps and symbols of baptism, How does it illustrate being a new creation?

  • What is the importance of the love mentioned in verse 14?

  • Who does those who live refer to in verse 15?

  • What does should no longer live for themselves, but for the One who died for them and was raised mean in verse 15?

  • What is 16 saying about the flesh? “So from now on we regard no one according to the flesh. Even if we have known Christ according to the flesh, we no longer know him in that way.”

  • What is 17 saying in response to disregarding the flesh in 16? Therefore if anyone is in Christ, this person is a new creation; the old things passed away; behold, new things have come.

  • Does “the newness” in verse 17 correspond to Christ?

 

Personal Reflection

2 Corinthians 5:14-21 HCSB;NASB

  • What about longevity? Have you endured something for a long length of time because of love for a person?
