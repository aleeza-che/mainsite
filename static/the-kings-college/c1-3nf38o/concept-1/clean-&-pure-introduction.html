Clean & Pure:

An Introduction to Soul Care

Introduction

Out of all of the scriptures of the bible, nothing quite captures the angst we may sometimes feel as believers when we are not fulfilling the potential we know is there within us, like in Romans 7, where Apostle Paul declares, "O wretched man that I am! who shall deliver me from the body of this death?" This can be especially frustrating in light of the goodness of God that we have experienced and our knowledge of the scriptures. The good we desire to do sometimes ridiculously eludes us and the motivation we once had seems to flap in the wind like a tattered and weather beaten flag, flickering here and there, but not standing at full mast as it once did. The last of the energy to pursue Kingdom like "good" Christians should, has fizzled out and left us struggling to make what seems like simple and straight forward decisions.

This study, based on biblical truths, suggests our issue may be that our current fuel is not efficient nor effective in producing lasting and consistent obedience; that current fuel being, feelings. If we operate by doing what we feel like doing, our flesh will make it impossible for us to experience the victory that is already ours through Christ Jesus. That is because feelings are the perfect fuel for the flesh and sub-optimal for the spirit. We need a fuel that goes beyond feelings, one that runs through the spiritual being, producing life, even when we find ourselves in the most perplexing of circumstances.

What if it isn't just the fuel that needs to be changed but the inner workings of the spirit man that need a tune up? Here enters soul care, the maintenance that we didn't want but truly need to be everything He's called us to be and we know we could be.

If we knew how to consistently operate outside of feelings, we'd be doing it already. The resistance we are facing in this spiritual paralysis mimics a body system overrun with toxicity. The organs of the spirit; the non-physical heart, mind and soul are bogged down with spiritual toxins like unresolved hurts, traumas, bad habits made out of good intentions, not to mention uncombated oppression from the enemy.

These things and more have zapped our strength and maybe worse, even skewed our perception of the Godhead, and ourselves. Jesus' sacrifice doesn't just offer salvation of the soul, healing of the body and resurrection life. He was also prophesied to be the Wonderful Counselor, so we can follow the command to love the Lord your God with all your heart, soul, mind and strength.

This is a study on how to allow yourself to be counseled directly by the Spirit of Christ.

For some, you may not be feeling like you are at your wits end, as it is described above, but you know there is more that you want to experience and can experience in this life with Christ. You have a hunger and thirst for righteousness and a desire to see the Kingdom of God expanded like never before and all you want to do is get in line. Preparation and God-readiness is required to subdue your dreams. There may be things that He needs to address that you may or may not know are therebefore you are unleashed on assignment. That requires His counsel. That requires soul care.

Although this not a self help on how to become your best you, that will inevitably end up happening if you allow Christ to counsel you and change you from a feelings-driven, sometimey-Christian, to one who rises to the occasion; not allowing sin or circumstance to rattle your eagle-eye view. You are not doing this to become a more productive child of God; you are doing it as an acceptance of a marriage proposal that will unify you into the family of God in a way that brings value to the family. The value that He has seen in you all along.

This Course uses the inductive Bible study method which draws it's conclusions based on what was studied from the text which is different from deductive bible study in which we bring our predisposed assumptions, whether original or learned to interpret the text being examined. It's main steps are observation, interpretation and application and it's main goal is to bring deeper and genuine understanding of scripture.

American Author and believer Kay Arthur, founder of Precept Ministries created a visual method for use in tandem with the inductive Bible study method. For this study, we've borrowed some of the techniques used in the Precept Bible Study Method. Find out more at Precept.org

Before we dive into the study, let's do the most important thing of all: let's invite God into this conversation through prayer. Take a moment to ask Him to speak to you through His written Word, the Bible.
