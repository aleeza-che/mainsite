Backdrop

This book of the Gospel of Christ written by John is very different from the other three gospels in many ways; too many to list in this backdrop. John—who is believed to be the closest disciple to Jesus and refers to himself in the writings as the one Jesus loved—focuses on the revelation of who Christ is in his entire identity; especially as the Son of God, and mentions the word believe almost 100 times. This book documents Jesus' 7 “I AM” statements, highlights 7 miracles done by Him, and confirms the witness of 7 persons declaring that He, indeed, is the Son of God. By this chapter, 2, Jesus has already been baptized by water and the Spirit, chosen his disciples and begun his public ministry—where He challenges many schools of thought.

For instance, the Pharisees were a self-appointed overseer group in Israel who set up their own laws, nitpicking and monitoring the activities of their fellow Israelis. They emerged in opposition to the Sadducees, the party of the Jewish high priesthood instated by the law of Moses. Pharisees rejected blind adherence to the law if it conflicted with human rationale or conscience. They believed1 “humans must use their reason in interpreting the Torah and [in] applying it to contemporary problems.” We drop in on Jesus' ministry right when he's about to have a conversation with a Pharisee named Nicodemus.

While reading this passage, we will put a cross for every reference to Jesus, including pronouns or titles; a triangle for every reference for God, including pronouns; a circle for every reference for the Holy Spirit; an up arrow for every reference to being born; a line under flesh, people, man or mankind, and an exclamation mark for believe and words like it.

4 Nicodemus *said to Him, “How can a person be born when he is old? He cannot enter his mother’s womb a second time and be born, can he?” 5 Jesus answered, “Truly, truly, I say to you, unless someone is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God. 6  That which has been born of the flesh is flesh, and that which has been born of the Spirit is spirit. 7  Do not be amazed that I said to you, ‘You must be born again.’ 8 The wind blows where it wishes, and you hear the sound of it, but you do not know where it is coming from and where it is going; so is everyone who has been born of the Spirit.”
9 Nicodemus responded and said to Him, “How can these things be?” 10 Jesus answered and said to him, “You are the teacher of Israel, and _yet_ you do not understand these things? 11 Truly, truly, I say to you, we speak of what we know and testify of what we have seen, and you _people_ do not accept our testimony. 12  If I told you earthly things and you do not believe, how will you believe if I tell you heavenly things? 13 No one has ascended into heaven, except He who descended from heaven: the Son of Man. 14 And just as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness , so must the Son of Man be lifted up, 15 so that everyone who believes will have eternal life in Him.
16 “For God so loved the world, that He gave His only Son, so that everyone who believes in Him will not perish, but have eternal life. 17For God did not send the Son into the world to judge the world, but so that the world might be saved through Him. 18 The one who believes in Him is not judged; the one who does not believe has been judged already, because he has not believed in the name of the only Son of God. 19  And this is the judgment, that the Light has come into the world, and people loved the darkness rather than the Light; for their deeds were evil. 20 For everyone who does evil hates the Light, and does not come to the Light, so that his deeds will not be exposed. 21 But the one who practices the truth comes to the Light, so that his deeds will be revealed as having been performed in God.”
 

Discuss

John 2:23-3:21 NASB
Please do your best to answer the questions as simply as you can based on the text given and not any prior knowledge you may have, even if it's biblical.

Questions with asterisks* may inquire about general definitions and meaning not found in the text, for those you may use your common knowledge.
  • What caused people to believe in Jesus' name at Passover? (2:23)
  • Using verses 2:23-25, What does it mean that they believed in His name?
  • In verse 3:5, what is Jesus referring to when He says "born of water and the Spirit"?
  • According to verse 3:8, Is it possible for someone to discern something about the Kingdom without being born of the Spirit?
  • What does it mean to hear the sound of the wind? (3:8)
  • If Nicodemus came to inquire of Jesus because of the signs he performed, why does Jesus say He doesn't "accept His testimony"? (3:11)
  • What does Jesus refer to Himself as in these scriptures? (3:13, 3:18)
  • According to 3:2, what does Nicodemus trust Jesus as? Why?
  • Does believing that Jesus can do miracles the same as believing in Him?
  • According to what Jesus says about judgment in 3:17-18, what could He be saying about Nicodemus?
  • According to verse 18, what must Nicodemus believe in order to discern more about the Kingdom?
  • Does the time of day Nicodemus comes have anything to do with what Christ is saying in 8:19, as a parallel?
  • What is the criteria for seeing (3:3) and entering (3:5) the Kingdom of God? Are they two different things?
  • Does the judgment discussed in 3:18-20 have to do with a person’s ability to believe? If not, then why is the judgment being passed?
  • What clear line is Jesus drawing in 3:6 about being able to interact with the Kingdom of God? “That which has been born of the flesh is flesh, and that which has been born of the Spirit is spirit.”
