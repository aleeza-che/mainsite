WELCOME TO THE KING'S COLLEGE

What is The King's College at Dependent Well?

The King’s College is not a platform of teachings. For many, being taught is not an issue. We have saturated ourselves as believers with teaching and somehow come up short in the execution of faith.

Matthew 23:8-10

The belief here at The King’s College is that the Deity/Godhead is fully capable and desires to do the work of sanctification in all believers, which will enable us to yield to YHWH God, thrive in character development, pursue and have success in purpose, and more. The King's College at Dependent Well is a platform for the cultivation of allowing the process of sanctification.

2 Thessalonians 2:13

VISION

to see generations burn with the desire to advance the Kingdom of YHWH God.

MISSION

To unblock the purposes, passions and purification processes in the lives of believers transforming them into yielders.

He’s given you the seed, He’s activating them, will you cultivate them for good growth?
Some 30, some 60, some 100 fold.
What is your goal?

We cover scripturally based topics at the King's College which may offer insight to help individuals navigate their way through spiritual conditions that may have them feeling stuck.

Bedstones of Faith

Body Building: Culture at The King's College

ROLES NOT RANK

We strongly believe that roles do not determine rank and would like to dismantle the culture that supports that way of thinking. Any leadership not rooted and grounded in servant-ship is not a value of The King's Kingdom. Luke 22:26-27

ACCOUNTABILITY 2.0 - SAFE SPACES

Groups are a big part of The King's College at Dependent Well. Accountability is not for nitpicking people's problems, it is for unpacking people's purpose. We regard no man after the flesh. We see them in the fullness of what God YHWH has revealed and at times they will not be able to see themselves in His light. We call them out for who they are, not what they are doing wrong. The King's College is a safe space. 2 Corinthians 5:16-21

Teams at the King's College

Avoiding th Trap of Isolation

There are many components to a healthy Spiritual life: encouragement, reading scripture, worship, community, rest, fasting, meditation on the Word, prayer, yielding to the Eternal Spirit and simply acknowledging the "here-ness" of the Deity. Community with genuine encouragement that is given by others, sparks spiritual joy which energizes and spurs the spirit on to greater. Teams serve the vision and mission of The King's College which combined are to advance the execution of faith in believers worldwide.
