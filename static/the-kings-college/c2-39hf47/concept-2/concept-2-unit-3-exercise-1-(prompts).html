Clean & Pure: An Introduction to Soul Care - Concept 2 - The Divorce

Romans 7:7-8:17 NASB

Make it Plain Exercise w/ Question Prompts

In writing it is easy to go back and forth between time and space to make a point.

For the sake of understanding, verse by verse, we will untangle what Paul is saying into short simple statements.

We will use questions to pull the simple statements from the text to get to the meat of the meaning.

Implicit Meaning: At times, the implicit meaning may have to be stated, which can get tricky, but the most important part is to keep it as simple and true to the text as possible.

There's a version of this Exercise without the question prompts. Make It Plain Exercise Without Question Prompts



Example
7
7 What shall we say then? Is the Law sin? Far from it! On the contrary, I would not have come to know sin except through the Law; for I would not have known about coveting if the Law had not said, “You shall not covet.”
7:7
Is the law sin?
Not: No.
Good answer: The law is not sin
What makes sin known?
Not: The Law.
Good answer: The law makes sin known.
