Backdrop

The word genesis means the origin. Genesis is the first book in the Christian scriptural library referred to as the Bible. It is also the first book in the collection of scripture of the Jewish faith called the Torah. This book is the explanation for the creation of things as we know it and also the origin of the people chosen to display who God is to the world. This book along with Exodus, Leviticus, Number and Deuteronomy, is believed to be written down by Moses–and prior to it's writing down–it's stories were passed down by word of mouth. We zone in on chapter 1 verse 26. The creation is unfolding with YHWH God having already made the earth, moon, stars, sea animals, birds and land creatures. He calls it good, then moves on to His last creation, mankind. To note: In Genesis chapter 2 starting from verse 2, once the overview of creation is given, the story back tracks and gives a more detailed account of the creation of mankind.

While reading this passage, we will put a triangle for every reference for God, His image, including pronouns, titles etc; a line for any reference to the flesh, including body and words like them; a heart around every reference for Adam or Eve directly, including descriptors and plural pronouns; a down arrow for serpent, death and words like it, and an up arrow for life, good, holy and words like it; a symbol like this for a command, actual commands, and words like it.
