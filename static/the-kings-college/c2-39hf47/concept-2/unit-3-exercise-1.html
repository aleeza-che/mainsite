Unit 3 - Exercise 1

Clean & Pure: An Introduction to Soul Care - Concept 2 - The Divorce

Romans 7:7-8:17 NASB

Make it Plain Exercise

In writing it is easy to go back and forth between time and space to make a point. For the sake of understanding, verse by verse, we will untangle what Paul is saying into short simple statements.

This may be challenging at first because every word will not be included in the simple short statements, just the meat of the meaning.

There's a version of this Exercise with question prompts to help bring out the short simple statements. Make It Plain Exercise With Question Prompts
Example
7
7 What shall we say then? Is the Law sin? Far from it! On the contrary, I would not have come to know sin except through the Law; for I would not have known about coveting if the Law had not said, “You shall not covet.”
7:7
The law is not sin
The law makes sin known
7
8 But sin, taking an opportunity through the commandment, produced in me coveting of every kind; for apart from the Law sin is dead.
7:8
Sin used the commandment to produce sin in a person
Sin is dead if there's no law
7
9 I was once alive apart from the Law; but when the commandment came, sin came to life, and I died;
7:9
A person is alive
The commandment was given
Sin came to life
The person died
7
10 and this commandment, which was to result in life, proved to result in death for me;
7:10
The law is intended to result in life
7
11 for sin, taking an opportunity through the commandment, deceived me, and through it, killed me.
7:11
Sin deceived the person
Sin killed the person
7
12 So then, the Law is holy, and the commandment is holy and righteous and good.
7:12
The law and it's commandments are holy
The commandments of the law are holy righteous and good



Continue through to the rest of the reading, Romans 7:13-8:17. We will use the simple sentences in another exercise to further bring clarity to the concept Paul is expounding on in this letter.
