Unit 3 - Exercise 2

Clean & Pure: An Introduction to Soul Care - Concept 2 - The Divorce

Romans 7:7-8:17 NASB

Set Your Mind Exercise

What if setting your mind on the Spirit and setting your mind on the flesh were a very simple practical concept? Let try an exercise.

Below are some prompts. I want you to close your eyes or open them what ever you prefer and actively start thinking about the prompt for 5 seconds.

Level 1

  • The gas station
  • The Dr's office
  • Tonight's dinner
  • A peaceful time of worship in a comfortable place
  • A crying baby
  • Traffic
  • Orange Juice

Level 2

This time along with thinking about it, use your imagination to visualize it in your mind in deeper detail. This time for 15 seconds.

  • Gas station: You arrive at the gas station because your car is on a quarter tank. The gas station is full and so you have to wait, but then just 2 seconds later, the closet car to you pulls off and you pull in.
  • The Dr's Office: You've been patiently waiting at the Dr's office for over 30 minutes and just as you're about to get up to leave, the nurse calls your name. She offer you an apology but also per their policy they hand you a $50 Visa gift card for having to wait more than 30 minutes.
  • Tonight's dinner: You already prepped all of your ingredients before you went off for work. The day was long and your winding down your street all ready to cook and enjoy. You walk in the house and your brother who was visiting has already anticipated your arrival and has a warm dinner waiting on the table.
  • A peaceful time of worship in a comfortable place: You've been very stressed lately and when you wake early on Sunday morning, you feel led to go to a church you used to attend years ago for bible study. You arrive and you feel God's loving embrace at the door. You experience a child like joy and time of worship that you've been longing for.
  • A crying baby: As you walk up the corridor to board your plane, you can already hear a fussing baby and accompanying mother cooing, trying her best. As you settle into your seat and find your headphones you notice a kind woman from first class ask to help the distressed mother who takes the baby and gently bounces him to sleep.

Level 3

In the room that you are in begin to imagine each object being specifically part of the plan of God concerning His kingdom. Objects are not just thing that you see, but things that you hear, smellâ€“the senses really, only you are not focusing on them as they are, but as reminders of keeping you mind set on the Spirit.

Look, listen or smell around, and any object that has grasped your attention, make a note in your mind or even better speak to the Holy Spirit in your mind about that object. You can, make a simple statement, i.e; the chair is white. Ask a question, i.e, what is that floral smell?, or acknowledge with gratitude, i.e; thank you for this pencil. Every time you do one write down the object just to keep track of how many. You will stop at 10.

What did you think of that exercise?

What if the whole earth reminded you of what God had said?

What if every time you took your mind off of considering everything around you through the lens of the Spirit, it was automatically back to the mindset of the flesh?

What would if take for you to train yourself on keeping your mind on the Spirit at all times?

Through this lens, does the bible verse below mean anything different to what you've thought it to mean?

Rejoice always, pray without ceasing, give thanks in all circumstances; for this is the will of God in Christ Jesus for you.
1 Thessalonians 5:16-18

Personal Reflection

  • What is your practice?
  • Is your mindset part of your Sabbath day?
  • What does it mean to you to keep it holy?
